Jerbot

Through this project, we are trying to explore an unconventional design for a biped robot that is devised from a desert rodent called Jerboa. This little rodent has come under the limelight of the scientific community in recent years. Jerboa's robust and unpredictable gait is considered a viable fix to the problems we face with the current humanoid type biped design

The complete package including demo videos can be found at Jerbot.

Prototype

The above gif demonstrates load test on our first prototype through which we did the mechanical validation of our hardware through PID control, load testing, disturbance rejection,etc.

Simulation

We built a custom Open AI Gym environment for experimenting with various Deep RL algorithms to learn optimal control strategies. Currently using Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO), to make the agent learn a robust gait sequence by Reward shaping in our simulated environment.

An demo of the robot in gazebo

We have used various kind of simulations to improve our design.We have used Towr (ROS based trajectory optimzation library for legged robots) to calculate and test our robot on various trajectories.Along with this we use Pybullet and Mujuco to test our robots.
